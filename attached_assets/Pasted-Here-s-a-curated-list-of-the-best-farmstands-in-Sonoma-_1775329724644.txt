Here’s a curated list of the best farmstands in Sonoma County:

🌿 THE PATCH
250 Second St., Sonoma · 707-849-7384
A 6-acre downtown Sonoma institution. Farmer Lazaro Calderon has been growing here for 25 years — a must-visit for summer heirloom tomatoes. ￼

🍎 OAK HILL FARM
Sonoma Highway & Arnold Drive, Glen Ellen
One of the most ambitious farms around with 45 acres in production. The farm store carries 200 varieties of fruits and vegetables, housemade honey, fresh eggs, soaps, fresh flowers, and a huge selection of pumpkins in fall. Open Wed–Sun, April through late December. ￼

🫒 SINGLE THREAD FARMS
2836 Dry Creek Road, Healdsburg · singlethreadfarms.com
A rare farmstand that supplies a Michelin-starred restaurant. Known for local excellence in produce and design. ￼

🍑 DRY CREEK PEACH & PRODUCE
Dry Creek Road, Healdsburg
A family-run farm growing organic tree-ripened, hand-picked peaches. The farmstand is open Wednesdays at noon from July to mid-September, offering just-picked produce, peach jam, and cobbler mix. ￼

🌱 SUNCATCHER FARM
4588 Bodega Ave., Petaluma · instagram.com/suncatcher_farm
Vibrant produce and herbal blends from a joyful roadside stand run by former sound engineer Patrick Krier. ￼

🥦 SONOMA FARM FRESH (Christopher Herrera)
7000 Petaluma Hill Road, Penngrove · sonomafarmfresh.com
Offers u-pick days, an honor stand, and seasonal farm dinners. A true regenerative farming experience. ￼

🌻 FLATBED FARM
13450 Highway 12, Glen Ellen · flatbedfarm.com
This Glen Ellen gem offers pickles, syrups, flowers, yoga, and wellness retreats. ￼

🍓 PETALUMA BOUNTY FARM
55 Shasta Ave., Petaluma · 707-765-8488 · petalumabounty.org
Grows vegetables, fruits, and flowers on a 3-acre ranch. The farmstand is open Thursdays from May to December, selling produce and blooms on a sliding scale with discounts available to those in need. ￼

🐝 BEES N BLOOMS
Santa Rosa
An 11-acre garden and farmstand probably best known for its lavender maze, at its peak mid-May to mid-July. Also offers classes in healthy soils, beekeeping, and building pollinator habitat. ￼

🍏 WALKER APPLES
10955 Upp Road, Sebastopol · 707-823-4310
The real deal — farmed by the Walker family for nearly 70 years, with 25+ varieties of apples. Harvest season begins in late July with Gravensteins and continues through fall. ￼

🫒 STARCROSS FAMILY CONFRATERNITY FARM
Annapolis (west county)
A monastic farming community producing cold-pressed extra virgin olive oil, olive leaf tea, and blackberry jam. The honor-system farmstand is open daily from 8 a.m. ￼

🌿 TENFOLD FARMSTAND
Petaluma (former 1895 Union Elementary School)
Organic vegetables and flowers with a community gathering focus — the historic schoolhouse hosts concerts, classes, and a small mercantile with baked goods and crafts. ￼

💡 Tip: Hours and seasonal availability vary widely — always check Instagram or the farm’s website before visiting. The Sonoma County Farm Trails map is also a great resource for finding additional stands across the county.​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​
