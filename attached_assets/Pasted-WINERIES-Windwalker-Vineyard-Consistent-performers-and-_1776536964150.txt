WINERIES

    Windwalker Vineyard — "Consistent performers" and "genuinely beautiful in harvest season." It's a real estate but the note is weak. I could write a better note if you want to keep it, or cut it — there's no shortage of Fair Play wineries.
    Bumgarner Winery — The overnight lodging angle is genuine and useful, but "excellent service and reasonable price point" reads like a Yelp summary. Worth keeping for the lodging context if the guide is about staying in the AVA, less so otherwise.
    Medeiros Family Wines — Live music and barrel tastings at Passport Weekend are the only specific details. For a weekend-visit context it may be fine; standalone it's thin.
    Lewis Grace Winery — "Over 25 award-winning wines" is the lead. That's a number, not an argument. The family story is interesting but the note doesn't make a specific case for the wine.
    Nello Olivo — The Godfather Experience is genuinely memorable and the winemaker (Marco Cappelli) has real credentials. But it's essentially an event winery — the main hook is once a year. Still has placeholder website.
    Chateau Davell — Real place, open, but the note reads more like an event venue (live music, French-inspired bites at Passport Weekend) than a wine destination. Placeholder website too.

FARMSTANDS

    Abel's Acres — Tiny Larsen Drive farm, generic applebill.com website, no distinction from the other Larsen Drive farms. Could argue it rounds out the corridor map; could argue it just adds noise.
    Denver Dan's — Same situation. Carson Road classic, but what specifically makes Denver Dan's worth the stop over Boa Vista or Delfino? The note doesn't answer that.
    El Dorado Orchards — North Canyon Road, generic note. "Quieter than the Carson Road farms" is its best argument.
    Smokey Ridge Farm & Winery — Listed as a farmstand but it's also a winery. The note is thin and the category is confused. Could be fixed or cut.

RESTAURANTS

    Henry's Steakhouse — It's inside Red Hawk Casino. It's open, it's decent, it's correctly located. But a casino steakhouse has nothing to do with El Dorado County's food identity. Up to you whether that's disqualifying.
