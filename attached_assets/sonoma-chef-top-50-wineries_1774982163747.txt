SONOMA CHEF — TOP 50 WINERIES WORTH YOUR TIME
Curated by region — from the Valley floor to the Sonoma Coast
==============================================================


RUSSIAN RIVER VALLEY
---------------------

01. Williams Selyem
    The benchmark Russian River Pinot. Allocation-heavy, appointment-only, and absolutely
    worth the effort. Old-vine purity at its finest.

02. Kosta Browne
    World-class Pinot Noir with a lush, precise house style. Get an appointment if you
    can — this is a bucket-list tasting.

03. Merry Edwards
    Old-vine Russian River Pinot royalty. Merry's wines are textbook Sonoma — elegant,
    cool-climate, endlessly drinkable.

04. Lynmar Estate
    Stunning Sebastopol estate with impeccable Pinot and Chardonnay. The grounds alone
    are worth the drive.

05. Gary Farrell
    Perched above the RRV with sweeping views. Single-vineyard Pinots that show exactly
    what this appellation can do.

06. Freeman Winery
    Intimate and unhurried. Redwood grove setting, cool wine cave, and Burgundian-inspired
    Pinots that reward attention.

07. Littorai
    Biodynamic, Burgundian, and deeply serious. Ted Lemon's coastal Pinot and Chardonnay
    are among California's finest.

08. Paul Mathew
    Meet the actual winemakers. Husband-and-wife operation in Graton pouring
    single-vineyard Pinots with old-world soul.

09. Pax Wine Cellars
    Downtown Sebastopol gem for Rhône-variety fanatics. The Syrah here is exceptional —
    buy a bottle and settle in.

10. Landmark Vineyards
    Historic RRV tasting room with estate Chardonnay and Pinot. A reliable, refined stop
    without the appointment fuss.

11. Domaine de la Rivière
    Relatively new but already collecting accolades. Pinot Noir and Chardonnay with
    old-world grace and genuine hospitality.


SONOMA VALLEY
-------------

12. Benziger Family Winery
    The essential biodynamic stop in Glen Ellen. The tram tour actually teaches you
    something, and the setting is gorgeous.

13. Imagery Estate Winery
    Artist-label wines, a working art gallery, and a patio that doesn't feel like a
    tourist trap. Underrated and worth it.

14. Gundlach Bundschu
    California's oldest family winery. "Gun Bun" does it right — beautiful grounds,
    great events, and an underrated Gewürztraminer.

15. Scribe Winery
    Hacienda vibes near Sonoma Plaza, excellent pét-nat and sparkling, and a food
    program built around the land.

16. Buena Vista Winery
    California's first premium winery (1857). The history alone is worth a visit,
    and the wines hold their own.

17. Chateau St. Jean
    1920s chateau grandeur in Kenwood. Award-winning wines, manicured grounds,
    and breathtaking mountain views.

18. B. Wise Winery
    A cave tasting experience unlike anything else — dramatic barrel-vaulted tunnels
    in the Moon Mountain District.

19. Repris Winery
    The Moon Mountain ATV tour is as memorable as the wine. Cabernet planted in the
    1880s. Rare and remarkable.

20. Three Sticks Wines
    Pre-Gold Rush adobe setting from 1842. Designed by Ken Fulk with the ambience
    of another era. Pinot and Chard.

21. Hamel Family Wines
    Certified biodynamic, dry-farmed since 2018. Cave tours, library tastings,
    Bordeaux-style blends. Appointment only.

22. Hanzell Vineyards
    Founded in 1953 by an ambassador inspired by Burgundy. Bird's-eye Sonoma Valley
    views and benchmark Chardonnay.

23. Viansa Winery
    Tuscan-inspired estate with Italian varietals, a deli marketplace, and picnic
    tables overlooking the valley.

24. Mayo Family Winery
    Absurd value for a deep-dive tasting in Kenwood. Many wines, attentive pours,
    zero pretension. A local secret.

25. St. Francis Winery
    Certified Sustainable family-owned estate. The food and wine pairing experience
    here is among Sonoma's best.

26. Coursey Graves
    French-inspired at 1,500 feet on Bennett Mountain. Panoramic views, Monet-style
    gardens, and luxury private tastings.


DRY CREEK VALLEY
----------------

27. Ferrari-Carano Vineyards
    Villa grandeur in Dry Creek. Stately rose gardens, early tastings, and a
    Fumé Blanc that earns its reputation.

28. Mauritson Wines
    Sixth-generation family Zinfandel. Honest, direct, and exactly what Dry Creek
    Valley should taste like.

29. Truett Hurst
    Biodynamic holistic farm with goats, sheep, chickens, and great Zinfandel.
    The eco-estate tour is a genuine delight.

30. Preston Farm & Winery
    No tour needed — winemaking operations visible in the courtyard. Feels like a
    European village bodega. Old-school cool.

31. Quivira Vineyards
    Biodynamic Dry Creek estate with certified-organic farming and a standout
    Zinfandel built on Rhône sensibility.

32. Dry Creek Vineyard
    The original Dry Creek label — and it shows. Consistent, honest, and the
    Fumé Blanc is a perennial benchmark.

33. Mounts Family Winery
    Tiny family operation where you might meet the winemaker. Rare varietals,
    bocce court, local vibes.

34. Ridge Lytton Springs
    Old-vine Zinfandel from one of California's most respected names. The Lytton
    Springs site is historic and essential.


ALEXANDER VALLEY
----------------

35. Jordan Winery
    Ivy-covered French château in Alexander Valley. The Cabernet is consistently
    excellent and the hospitality is real.

36. Francis Ford Coppola Winery
    Part winery, part resort — pools, bocce, Hollywood memorabilia. Great for
    groups and a genuinely fun afternoon.

37. Stonestreet Estate
    Mountain estate Cabernet at its Sonoma best. The Alexander Mountain terroir
    produces power with elegance.

38. Chalk Hill Estate
    1,300-acre estate with stunning views, warm hospitality, and terroir-driven
    Chardonnay that punches above its price.

39. Medlock Ames
    Modern rural-hipster done right. Founded by two college friends in 1998 with
    organic farming at the center.

40. Banshee Wines
    New Alexander Valley tasting room near Healdsburg. Pinot and vinyl — select
    your record, pour your wine, vibe out.


HEALDSBURG
----------

41. Seghesio Family Vineyards
    The Healdsburg Zinfandel standard. Multi-generational, Italian-rooted, and pours
    with the ease of family confidence.

42. Locals Tasting Room
    One tasting room, seven small producers. The smartest first stop in Healdsburg
    for scoping what you want to pursue.

43. Bricoleur Vineyards
    Stunning RRV estate south of Healdsburg. Picnic wagons, bocce, rose gardens,
    and consistently elegant wines.

44. J Vineyards & Winery
    Top California sparkling wine producer. The Bubble Room five-course pairing is
    a legitimate destination dining experience.

45. Dry Creek Kitchen Wine Bar
    Charlie Palmer's downtown Healdsburg bar — wines by the glass from across the
    county with food that matches.

46. Zina Lounge
    Sunset patio, fireplace inside, cheese boards, and three Healdsburg-area labels
    in one spot. The evening wind-down.


CARNEROS / PETALUMA
-------------------

47. The Donum Estate
    50+ monumental sculptures on 200 acres plus world-class Pinot and Chardonnay.
    Art and wine in full Carneros elegance.

48. Donum Winery
    Cool-climate Carneros estate straddling the Sonoma-Napa line. Regenerative
    farming, friendly donkeys, fine Pinot.

49. Schug Carneros Estate
    German-born winemaker Walter Schug's legacy estate. Benchmark Carneros Pinot
    and Chardonnay in a quiet setting.

50. Region Wine Bar
    Downtown Sebastopol's finest pour. 50+ wines by the taste via self-pour machines.
    The ideal first stop before any winery day.
